# Target Personas - Sidekick Strategies

## Overview
Sidekick Strategies serves businesses that have invested in HubSpot but aren't realizing its full potential. These personas represent the primary decision-makers and users we help.

---

## Persona 1: The Overwhelmed Business Owner

### Profile
- **Role**: CEO, Founder, or Business Owner
- **Company Size**: 10-100 employees
- **HubSpot Status**: Has HubSpot but underutilizing it
- **Decision Power**: High - signs contracts

### Pain Points
- Invested significant money in HubSpot but not seeing ROI
- Team isn't adopting the platform consistently
- Feels like they're missing something obvious
- Too busy running the business to learn HubSpot properly
- Frustrated by complexity of the platform

### Goals
- Get real, measurable value from HubSpot investment
- Empower team to use tools effectively without constant handholding
- Stop feeling overwhelmed by technology
- Have systems that work while they focus on growth

### Qualifying Language
> "We have HubSpot but we're not really using it"
> "My team doesn't log anything in the CRM"
> "I know we're not using it right"
> "We paid for all these features but..."
> "I don't have time to figure this out"

### How We Help
- HubSpot audit to identify quick wins
- Team training for adoption
- Strategic consulting on what features actually matter
- Ongoing sidekick support for questions

### Voice Adjustments
- Empathetic to time constraints
- Focus on ROI and practical outcomes
- Avoid technical jargon
- Emphasize team enablement

## Persona 2: The Growth-Focused Sales Leader

### Profile
- **Role**: VP of Sales, Sales Director, Sales Manager
- **Company Size**: 20-500 employees
- **HubSpot Status**: Using Sales Hub, wants more from it
- **Decision Power**: High for sales tools, influences overall

### Pain Points
- Pipeline visibility issues - can't forecast accurately
- Manual data entry eating into selling time
- Inconsistent follow-up processes across team
- Forecasting feels like guesswork
- Reps not using the CRM properly

### Goals
- Automate repetitive administrative tasks
- Better pipeline insights and visibility
- Consistent, repeatable sales processes
- Accurate forecasting for leadership
- Higher team productivity and quota attainment

### Qualifying Language
> "We need better visibility into our pipeline"
> "My reps spend too much time on admin work"
> "Our process isn't consistent across the team"
> "I can't trust the numbers in HubSpot"
> "Forecasting is basically guessing"

### How We Help
- Sales Hub optimization and configuration
- Pipeline and deal stage setup
- Sequence and workflow automation
- Custom reporting dashboards
- Sales team training and adoption

### Voice Adjustments
- Results and metrics focused
- Speak to efficiency and productivity
- Use sales-specific terminology appropriately
- Emphasize competitive advantage

### Qualifying Language

> "Customers keep asking the same questions"
> "We have no visibility into satisfaction"
> "Everything lives in someone's head"
> "We need to scale without hiring more people"

### How We Help
- Service Hub implementation and configuration
- Ticket workflow and SLA setup
- Knowledge base creation and organization
- Customer feedback and NPS surveys
- Support automation and chatbots

### Voice Adjustments
- Empathetic to the support struggle
- Focus on customer experience outcomes
- Speak to scalability and efficiency
- Acknowledge the human element of support

## Persona Usage Guide

### For Content Creation
When writing content, consider which persona(s) you're addressing:
- Use their language and pain points in headlines
- Address their specific goals in body copy
- Include qualifying questions they'd recognize
- Tailor CTAs to their decision-making process

### For Sales Conversations
- Listen for qualifying language during discovery
- Match your approach to their communication style
- Focus on the outcomes that matter to them
- Use case studies from similar roles/situations

### For Service Delivery
- Understand their perspective on the engagement
- Anticipate their questions and concerns
- Deliver value in ways they'll recognize
- Measure success by their goals, not just tasks
